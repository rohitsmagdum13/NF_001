## 1. Water Reform

### New South Wales - Water Reform

**New Plan - Water Sharing Plan for the Lachlan Unregulated River Water Sources**

The Water Sharing Plan for the Lachlan Unregulated River Water Sources 2026 No. 65 (NSW) has been made under the authority of the Water Management Act 2000 No. 92 (NSW).

The Plan:

- provides for the requirements for water;
- provides for the limits to the availability of water;
- provides for the operation of water allocation accounts and managing access licences; and
- provides for other and related matters.

The Plan commenced on 27 February 2026.
*(Source: Lawlex Legislative Alert & Premium Research)*

## 2. Urban Water Supply and Use

### Victoria - Urban Water Supply and Use

**Latest Weekly Water Update**

Melbourne Water has made available its latest Weekly water storage and use update for the week ending 5 March 2026, which provides information on dam storage levels, average catchment rainfall, flow into dams and water use.
*(Source: Melbourne Water)*

**Melbourne's Summer Water Storage Report: Every Drop Counts as Levels Fall**

Melbourne Water has announced that "[it] is encouraging Melburnians to reduce water use as storage levels close summer at 69%, the lowest since 2020 and more than 11 percentage points below last year".

According to Melbourne Water, "[s]torages began the summer at 75%, before falling to 70% by 28 February [2026], a net decrease of 5% or 99.5 billion litres. At the same time last year, storage was at 81% [and] [w]hile streamflow into catchments this summer was marginally above the 30-year average, the broader picture tells a different story".

Melbourne Water's media release (9 March 2026)
*(Source: Melbourne Water)*

**Blue Green Algae Red Alert at Wah Wah Main**

Murrumbidgee Irrigation has announced that "[it] has identified levels of blue-green algae which currently exceed the livestock guidelines in Wah Wah Main (Barden's Regulator) [and] [t]here are no algal guidelines for irrigation use".

According to Murrumbidgee Irrigation, "[they] are committed to monitoring blue-green algae levels in ... storages, irrigation channels and drains within [their] area of operations, in accordance with a contingency strategy developed by the Murrumbidgee Regional Algal Coordinating Committee (RACC) - a body established by the NSW Government".

Murrumbidgee Irrigation's media release (6 March 2026)
*(Source: Murrumbidgee Irrigation)*

### Western Australia - Collection

**Expanded Desalination Plant to Help Secure Pilbaras Water Supply**

The Water Corporation has announced that "Western Australians living in the West Pilbara will benefit from long-term water security ... the Cook Government's $606 million investment which will double the $1.1 billion Dampier Seawater Desalination Plant's (DSDP) capacity".

According to Water Corporation, "[s]tage 1 is being built by Rio Tinto [and] [w]ith the addition of Stage 2, the newly expanded plant will ... supply eight billion litres of water to the Pilbara annually, including providing drinking water to more than 10,000 homes in Karratha, Wickham, Roebourne, and Point Sampson".

Water Corporation's media release (4 March 2026)
*(Source: Water Corporation)*
